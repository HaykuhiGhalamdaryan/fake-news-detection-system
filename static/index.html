About TruthLens

A multi-signal fake news detection system built for academic research.

What is TruthLens?

TruthLens is a fake news detection system that combines machine learning models, semantic fact-checking, and linguistic analysis to assess the credibility of news claims and articles.

Built as a Bachelor's thesis project, it uses an ensemble of two fine-tuned RoBERTa models alongside Wikipedia, NewsAPI, and DuckDuckGo for multi-source evidence retrieval.

How It Works

1

Text Chunking

Long articles are split into overlapping 300-word chunks so no content is silently truncated by the model's 512-token limit.

2

ML Ensemble Scoring

Two fine-tuned RoBERTa models (hamzab + jy46604790) score each chunk independently. Scores are aggregated with position and confidence weighting.

3

Text Feature Analysis

8 surface-level signals are detected: ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, clickbait phrases, hyperbolic language, vague attribution, and more.

4

Multi-Source Fact Checking

Wikipedia, NewsAPI, and DuckDuckGo are queried in parallel. Sentence Transformers compute semantic similarity between the claim and found evidence.

5

Contradiction Detection

The claim's negation is also scored against the evidence. Geographic mismatch penalties catch wrong-entity matches (e.g., "Armenia" city vs. country).

6

Hybrid Verdict

A two-track decision engine combines all signals: fact-check driven for verifiable claims, ML-driven for opinion and manipulation detection.

Verdict Scale

TrueStrong fact support + low fake probability
Likely TrueModerate evidence of truth
UncertainConflicting or insufficient signals
Likely FakeElevated fake probability + manipulation signals
FakeHigh fake probability + weak/contradicting evidence

Limitations

• Models are English-only

• Short claims may score conservatively

• Satire is difficult to detect reliably

• Very recent events may lack Wikipedia coverage
